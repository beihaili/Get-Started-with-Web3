How this would move

  1. 1The app prepares the UserOperation from the user's intent, target, nonce, calldata, and gas budget.
  2. 2A bundler simulates validation before accepting the operation into a UserOperation mempool.
  3. 3EntryPoint checks the account validation path and makes sure the operation can pay.
  4. 4If the smart account is new, the factory deploys it before execution.
  5. 5EntryPoint calls the account, and the account executes the encoded action.
  6. 6Unused gas is refunded and the bundler beneficiary is paid from the account or paymaster deposit.

Learning boundary

No wallet, private key, signature, raw address, or transaction is sent to a server, bundler, or analytics endpoint.

Paymaster sponsorship can improve onboarding, but production systems need budgets, abuse controls, allowlists, and clear failure states.

EIP-7702 authorization can change how an EOA executes. Treat unknown delegates like high-risk approvals.
