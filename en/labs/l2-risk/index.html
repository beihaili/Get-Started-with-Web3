Scenario modifiers

  • +1 because this direction adds extra finality or message-shape complexity.

Finality path

  • Wait for source-chain confirmation before treating the route as started.
  • Optimistic withdrawals may require a challenge window before L1 execution.
  • A final claim or execution transaction may still be needed on the destination chain.

Trust assumptions

  • Canonical L1/L2 bridge contracts and their upgrade controls.
  • Fraud-proof or challenge mechanisms and honest watcher availability.
  • Governance, multisig, or security council upgrade keys.

Message path

  • Asset route locks or burns on the source side, then mints or unlocks representation elsewhere.
  • Canonical messenger passes withdrawal or deposit messages through rollup contracts.

Failure modes

  • Withdrawal delay, watcher failure, or unresolved challenge can delay exits.
  • Sequencer downtime can delay inclusion, withdrawals, or proof generation.
  • Admin or governance upgrade keys can change bridge or rollup behavior.

Before using a real route

  • Prefer official/canonical bridges for large or first-time movements.
  • Read the withdrawal delay and make sure the exit path is available.
  • Send a small test amount before moving meaningful value.
